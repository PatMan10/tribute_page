Shawn "Jay-Z" Carter

The man who made it against the odds.

Jay-Z chilling on a speaker.

Here is a time line of Jay-Z's life

  • 1969 - Shawn Corey Carter was Born in Brooklyn, New York.

  • Early years - Shawn was raised in Marcy Houses, a housing projects for poor African-Americans. His father, Adnis Reeves, abandoned the family leaving Gloria Carter to raise her three kids, including Shawn, by herself. Young Shawn, without a father figure and a struggling mother of three, was forced to turn to street life to survive and make ends meet.

    He attended several different high schools but did not graduate and during this time period he sold crack cocaine to support the family. During his years selling drugs he was shot at on three occasions, but he was never hit. His time in the drug game taught him a wide variety of valuable skills that would serve him later in his life.

    Shawn displayed a talent and passion for rap music from an early age. When he wasn't on the streets hustling, he would bang out beats and rap to them for hours on end. He even went so far as to do this late at night waking up his family members on many occasions. His mother took note of this passion and bought him a boom box so he could practice more and hopefully stay out of the streets. He continued to practice rapping for years and became known as "Jazzy" around his neighbourhood, this was the inception of Jay-Z.

  • 1995-1997 - Jay-Z, a grown man now, fed up with the ills of the drug game decided he wanted to pursue a career in rap music full-time. Tried to get a recording deal with several record companies but failed. Decided to start his own record company Roc-A-Fella Records.

    In the early days he and his associates sold records out of the trunk of his car. Eventually he got a distribution deal with Priority and released his debut studio album named Reasonable Doubt. Album would later be included in Rolling Stone's "500 Greatest Albums of All Time" as No. 248 and eventually reach platinum status.

    Got a new distribution deal with Def Jam in 1997, Jay-Z released his follow-up In My Lifetime, Vol. 1., it sold better than the previous effort. In My Lifetime, Vol. 1 earned platinum status in the United States.

  • 1998-2000 - Jay-Z releases two more albums Vol. 2 Hard Knock Life and The Dynasty. Hard Knock Life (Ghetto Anthem) becomes the most successful song he ever made. Vol. 2 Hard Knock Life became the most successfully album he ever made going 5 x Platinum in the US. Jay-Z and Co. found clothing brand Rocawear.

  • 2001-2002 - Jay-Z drops two more albums The Blueprint 1 and 2. Beefs with Nas. The Blueprint goes 2 x Platinum in the US and wins a Grammy award. Gets sued for stabbing record producer Lance Riviera, sentenced to three years probation, no jail time.

  • 2003-2005 - goes on holiday in south of france. drops one more album, the black album. establishes the 40/40 night club. "retires" from rap music. becomes president and ceo of def jam records.

  • 2005-2007 - Makes a comeback with two more albums, Kingdom Come and American Gangster. Hosts a major concert called "I Declare War". He ends beef with Nas. Took over Rocawear brand and invested in Carols's Daughter.

  • 2008-2011 - Marries Beyonce Knowles. Resigns as president and CEO of Def Jam records. Releases two more albums, The Blueprint 3 and Watch the Throne. Performs at major British concert. Receives criticism from Noel Gallagher. Publishes his memoir Decoded.

  • 2012-2016 - Drops the album Magna Carta Holy Grail. Performs in several more concerts and tours. Launches his own sports agency Roc Nation Sports. Acquired Aspiro, a Norwegian tech company, for $56 million.

  • 2017-2018 - Releases a solo album titled 4:44, and another joint album with Beyonce titled Everything is Love.
